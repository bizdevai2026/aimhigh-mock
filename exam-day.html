Exam-Day Drill

What top students do in the room

Six moves, every paper. Stick this on your bedroom door.

  1. 1
    Read the WHOLE paper first. Spend 1-2 minutes scanning every page before you write. Spot the easy ones, mentally pre-warm the hard ones.
  2. 2
    1 minute per mark. No more. 6-mark question = 6 minutes max. If you go over, FLAG IT and come back. Bank the easy marks first.
  3. 3
    Easy questions FIRST. Build confidence + bank marks before tackling the hard stuff. The hard ones get easier when you've already got 30 marks in the bag.
  4. 4
    Stuck for 90 seconds? Flag, leave, come back. Don't burn 5 minutes staring at one question. Circle it, move on, the answer often appears while you're doing something else.
  5. 5
    BUG every question. Box the command word (describe / explain / compare). Underline what it asks for. Glance back after writing — did you actually answer THAT?
  6. 6
    Last 5 minutes — the check pass. Re-read every command word. Check units (cm, m², kg). Check you answered all parts (a, b, c). Easy marks live here.

Mark schemes are the template. Examiners reward STRUCTURE more than brilliance.

If you blank: write ANYTHING about the topic. The act of writing unfreezes the brain. Examiners only mark what's on the page.
