Four representative diagrams from visuals.js, each shown three ways: as it is now, redrawn in a friendly illustrated style (Bitesize-feel), and redrawn in a technical refined style (cobalt-premium). All assets shown are originally drawn — none lifted from third-party sources. Pick one direction and the rest of the 17 visuals get redrawn to match.

Animal cell vs plant cell

Geography · rivers

River meander — erosion vs deposition

Current

Two arrows + two labels. Doesn't really show what a meander LOOKS like or feels like. Verdict: teaches the term, not the thing.

A · Friendly illustrated

Same two concepts, but the river LOOKS like a river — banks, fish, grass tufts, sun. Erosion and deposition are colour-coded chips with arrows pointing at the right banks. Reads as: "geography lesson, not a textbook page".

B · Technical refined

Same content, but read like a field-survey map: faint grid, flow arrow, callout boxes off to the side, scale bar. Reads as: "this is what real geographers see".

Maths · graphs

Distance-time graph — fast vs slow vs stationary

Current

Three bare lines on bare axes. Correct, but doesn't help a kid feel what "fast" or "stationary" means visually. Verdict: abstract.

A · Friendly illustrated

A rocket for fast, a walking stick-figure for slow, a sleeping dot for stationary. Reads as: "speed has a feeling, not just a number".

B · Technical refined

Same lines, but with subtle area-fills, endpoint markers, chip labels, axis units, and a faint grid. Reads as: "exam-quality graph".

History · medieval

Feudal pyramid — King → Barons → Knights → Peasants

Current

Stacked tiles with labels. Functional pyramid but no visual storytelling. Verdict: a bullet list in triangle form.

A · Friendly illustrated

Each tier has tiny illustrated people doing what they actually did — crowned king, plumed barons, helmeted knights with swords, dozens of peasants. Reads as: "you can SEE the social order".

B · Technical refined

One unified pyramid with a coral-to-cobalt gradient, numbered tier chips, population scale per tier, exchange flow ↑↓ on either side. Reads as: "this is an analytical model, not a chart".

How to read these

  1. Current column lifts the SVG straight out of visuals.js. No filter, no flattering — that's exactly what a kid sees.
  2. Option A — Friendly illustrated commits to characters, soft colour, hand-drawn-feel outlines, and explanatory metaphors (rocket = fast, grass tufts = banks, tiny knights with swords). Bitesize-feel without copying any Bitesize asset.
  3. Option B — Technical refined stays diagrammatic but pushes hard on craft: gradients, callout lines extending outside the subject, axis units, tier chips, scale bars. Cobalt-premium feel that matches the chosen palette.
  4. Both options are originally drawn SVG. No imagery is sourced from Bitesize or any other site. For topics where a real photo would help (e.g. a real meander, real cells under a microscope), I'd separately source CC-licensed imagery from Wikimedia Commons / Pexels / Unsplash and pair it with the chosen diagram style — that's an additive layer, not a replacement.
